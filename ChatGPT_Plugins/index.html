An Early Review of ChatGPT Plugins

发表于 2023-05-20 分类于 Reviews 阅读次数： 本文字数： 15k
Navigating the Potentials and Limitations of AI Interaction Through OpenAI's ChatGPT Plugins


OpenAI recently announced that all ChatGPT Plus account holders will have access to ChatGPT plugins, starting from May 19. As a Plus user myself, I finally got to experience the plugin feature today. Understandably, I’m quite excited and can’t wait to delve into the capabilities these plugins offer.

Interestingly, I had early access to the web browsing feature. Initially, using GPT-4 was a challenge as it would frequently freeze and become unresponsive. However, I subsequently applied to join the plugin developer waitlist on the official website. Shortly after, I received the plugins, and GPT-4 seemed to return to working condition.

Introduction

In my view, the plugins for ChatGPT are a set of API interfaces. They enable ChatGPT to interact with other software, gathering information or executing tasks through them. While we already have an array of graphical interfaces for human-computer interaction, it’s difficult for large language models (LLMs) to operate them, since they’re primarily designed for visual beings, like humans. Nevertheless, LLMs have their own unique method of interacting with software, a method that’s arguably more efficient than human intervention. This is precisely where plugins and APIs come into play.

OpenAI has also officially released two plugins: web browsing and a code interpreter. I believe the latter could be incredibly powerful, potentially paving the way for a new form of workflow, turning everyone into a Python programmer through the use of natural language. I’m eager to gain access to this feature in the future.

In order to utilize these plugins, we first need to enable this feature in the settings. Then, a plethora of plugins can be found in the plugin store. However, the design of the plugin store leaves something to be desired as it lacks handy features like search or classification. As of May 18, there are over 70 plugins available. Additionally, it seems that OpenAI currently only permits developers to install unverified plugins by entering the website domains.

Plugins

In this section, I will highlight some of the plugins I’ve experimented with, and rank them based on their practicality. As I am based in China, certain local services, such as shopping at local markets, are inaccessible, which prompts me to primarily focus on productivity. I will update this blog post as and when I explore more plugins.

Highly recommended:

VoxScript

VoxScript proves to be an excellent tool for searching YouTube transcripts. It can help save substantial time by eliminating the need to watch lengthy, non-informative videos.

For example, when prompted with:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YBBnQmRcRI4 summarize this video for me and evaluate it

ChatGPT sends a request to VoxScript and receives a response containing comprehensive video information, including a Video Description and a Video Transcript. Interestingly, the transcript spans five pages, leading ChatGPT to make four additional requests to retrieve the full transcript.

KAYAK

KAYAK functions well for travel planning, assisting with flight and hotel bookings.

For instance, if you ask,

I live in (the city), China and am planning a 3-day trip to Sydney, Australia during my summer vacation. Could you please help me plan the trip and estimate the costs?

ChatGPT proactively seeks further details,

than aiding in the discovery of flights and accommodation that meet your requirements, while also calculating an estimated cost. Its attention to detail, even in aspects you may not have mentioned, is commendable.

Link Reader

Despite web browsing and webpage reading being available on the web version of GPT, plugins like Link Reader offer similar functionalities and even read PDFs and docs. It should be noted, however, that it does not recognize images and only provides details such as resolution.

You need to supply a link and request information. Importantly, ChatGPT does not support file upload, so you’ll need to upload your file elsewhere and provide the corresponding link.

For instance, you could ask:

Can you summarize this PDF for me: https://linkforyour.pdf

Wolfram

Wolfram, a renowned AI software, enables ChatGPT to access advanced computation, math, visualizations, and real-time data to solve a variety of questions. It is indispensable for any mathematical calculations within ChatGPT.

Although I haven’t utilized it yet, I’ll certainly give it a shot when the need arises.

Tasty Recipes

The Tasty Recipes plugin works flawlessly when tasked with advanced recipe requests.

For instance, when I prompted,

Recommend some recipes for my birthday party

ChatGPT took the initiative to ask for more specific details

than it provided a well-rounded menu. It suggested Appetizers, Main Courses, Desserts, and even Cocktails that fit my requirements seamlessly. You can also ask for ingredients for each recipe or the preparation instructions subsequently. I’m greatly impressed by its performance and will undoubtedly turn to it for my future recipe needs.

LikeWise

The LikeWise plugin offers tailored recommendations for TV shows, movies, and podcasts. It excels when suggesting content based on your personal preferences and boasts a vast database, ensuring there’s always something to catch your interest. However, despite mentioning books in its description, its book database appears insufficient.

For example, when I prompted,

Recommend some popular TV shows like The Glory

ChatGPT provided an insightful response.

Usable:

World News

The World News plugin serves up global news along with source links. However, I have reservations about the quality of its news, which seems somewhat mediocre and non-representative. In my view, relying on this plugin for news updates might not enhance your efficiency but rather consume your time with a deluge of middling news stories.

OpenTable

OpenTable is a useful plugin for finding and booking restaurants. Once you input details like the date, time, location, and number of people, it provides links to various restaurants for making reservations. However, during my test, when I requested restaurants in Sydney, it incorrectly suggested five eateries located in Washington, an obvious error that needs rectification.

Expedia

This comprehensive travel plugin, Expedia, offers more features than KAYAK, including flights, accommodations, local activities, and resorts. Despite providing me with a list of local hotels, their prices were prohibitively high, making it less useful for budget-conscious travelers.

Furthermore, it recommended some lesser-known local activities. Note that ChatGPT takes three messages to deliver these varied recommendations, requiring you to prompt “continue” each time.

Speak

The Speak plugin assists with language translation, providing not just literal translations but also alternative phrases and sample conversations. Although it can be useful in certain circumstances, it does require the patience to pose the question and wait for a response.

Show me

This plugin aids in the creation of diagrams, like mind maps. All you need to do is ask ChatGPT to depict a concept or system, and it presents a visual representation along with a link for further editing. This is incredibly helpful for those unfamiliar with diagram creation. However, it has room for improvement, like in situations where it drew two arrows pointing to the same box.

Less Useful:

Instacart

Instacart assists in generating shopping lists based on recipe suggestions from ChatGPT. However, the website did not permit access from my location, and the service is not available in my country. It’s important to note that several other plugins only provide local service in specific regions, rendering them ineffective outside of these areas.

There’s an AI For That

This plugin helps you find AI tools from a large database. You can look for specific tools by asking the AI about your requirements. However, it doesn’t offer any reviews or evaluations of these tools, which might lead to a time-consuming process of trying each one individually. In such cases, a Google search or user recommendations might be more efficient.

PromptPerfect

PromptPerfect is a plugin designed to help refine your prompts. I understand that some individuals, particularly beginners, might struggle with formulating prompts. However, I believe this technology still has a long way to go. There’s a level of nuanced understanding required in prompt engineering, an area where AI can’t quite discern human intentions fully yet.

To use this plugin, simply add the word ‘perfect’ before your prompt, and it will rephrase your prompt in a more complex manner. For instance:

Perfect: Write a fable about the relationship between today's artificial intelligence and human beings

The plugin rewrote my prompt as follows:

It can be difficult to determine which version — the original or rephrased — would yield a better response from ChatGPT. The original prompt sought a story portraying AI as initially distrusted by humans but eventually becoming their saviors. The revised prompt, more akin to a fable, narrates how Dr. Turing developed an AI called Ada, teaching humans not to fear AI because it reflects the traits of its users.

Therefore, I recommend writing clear and detailed prompts yourself. Use this plugin sparingly, unless your goal is a highly simplified sentence.

Zapier

In my opinion, the Zapier plugin isn’t worth it, primarily due to the requirement of a Zapier account registration and a monthly fee of $49 USD. Despite its claim to facilitate interaction with over 5000 apps, it necessitates manual action addition, which seems to defeat the purpose of AI assistance.

Future

Thus far, I have yet to explore all the plugins, so their full potential remains somewhat of a mystery. Considering that you can combine up to three plugins and facilitate their interaction with one another, it makes one wonder what novel creations can be brought to life.

Frankly, my assumption is that all these plugins are currently in the alpha testing stage and highly experimental. I’m reserving my expectations about their prospects due to their inherent restrictions and closed nature. As interfaces allowing LLMs to access various software, these plugins will undoubtedly evolve. However, I view them as merely a temporary solution for GPT-4 rather than the ultimate path forward. It seems plausible to suggest that our current stage of human intelligence is just a fleeting phase in the grand evolution of intellect.
